Cinnamon Buns

  • Tips and Tricks
  • Dough Ingredients
  • Icing Ingredients
  • Dough Directions
  • Icing Directions

Tips and Tricks

  • Using fresh yeast will make the buns even more fluffy.
  • Fluff up flour then spoon it into a dry measuring cup. Level off the top.
  • Use butter instead of oil for more flavor. Butter also helps keep the shaped dough together when shaping the buns.
  • For a different flavor, you can fill the dough with a combination of sugar and poppy seeds instead of cinnamon.

Dough Ingredients

  • 14 g/1.5 tbsp active dry yeast or 50 g fresh yeast if available
  • 175 ml/3/4 cup warm water
  • 3 Tbsp sugar
  • 250 ml/1 cup warm milk
  • 1 egg large
  • 85 ml/1/3 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 700 g/5 â…“ cups strong bread flour
  • 125 g/1/2 cup butter unsalted and softened
  • 100 g/1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 Tbsp cinnamon
  • 1 egg for brushing

Cream Cheese Icing Ingredients (Optional)

  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 tbsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup powdered sugar

Dough Directions

  1. In a small bowl combine the warm water, yeast, and sugar and let it stand for 5 minutes. Then add the warm milk, oil, salt, and 1 egg. Mix with a fork.
  2. Add the liquid to the flour, mix until rough dough forms, then take it out of the bowl onto a floured surface and start kneading until the smooth and elastic dough is formed for approximately 8-10 minutes. If using a stand mixer, knead using a dough hook attachment for 1 minute on low speed, then switch to medium and kneed for 4 minutes longer.
  3. Take the dough out of the bowl and rub the bowl with vegetable oil, then return the dough to the bowl, cover with plastic wrap and a towel and let it rise for 45 minutes to 1 hour in a warm place until doubles in size.
  4. Meanwhile, preheat the oven to 350F. Mix the softened butter with sugar and cinnamon with a mixer. Set aside until later.
  5. Take the dough out of the bowl and place it on a floured surface. Dust the top with a bit of flour and roll out into a large rectangle until the dough is no more than 1 inch thick. Dab the sheet of dough with the butter mix all over, then spread in an even layer going all the way to the edges.
  6. Roll the dough up starting with one of the longer sides and work in a typewriter motion, keeping a tight roll. Once it's rolled up, push ends in slightly to make them a little more uniform then slice into 12 equal-sized rolls.
  7. Place each cinnamon bun 2 inches apart. Cover with a tea towel and let them rise for 30 minutes.
  8. Before baking brush them with a beaten egg and bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes until golden. Cool on a wire rack.

Icing Directions (Optional)

  1. In a medium bowl with an electric hand mixer, cream together 4 Tbsp softened butter and 4 oz cream cheese.
  2. Beat in 1/2 Tbsp vanilla then add 1 cup powdered sugar and continue mixing on high speed until fluffy (3-4 min), scraping down the bowl as needed.
